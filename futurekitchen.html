LUKE SIMMONS RESUME WORK ABOUT ME

Caesarstone partnered with students from Pratt Institute, under the guidance of Marc Thorpe, to create a concept of what the kitchen will look like in the year 2050. Our concept addresses societal, cultural, and environmental changes that will become prevalent in the near future. These include a world population of 10 billion, a vast influx of people moving to cities; due to these factors home gardening has become mainstream. Technology has improved, yet humans need to reconnect with their roots; the hearth has re-emerged as the center unifying element of all social and family life. The design was debuted at Wanted Design, where it won multiple awards including Best of Wanted Design from Surface Magazine, Top 10 at Wanted Design by Dezeen, Best of NYCxDesign from Metropolis, and was featured on Design Milk's Instagram.

Before beginning designing the concept, our team was divided into five research categories: composting, smart technology, food storage, water systems, and robotic farming. Each team was responsible for coming up with a futuristic way to incorporate their research into our final design concept.

My team was responsible for the smart technology research. Our focus was to tie all of the internal systems together in the central hub. Using this as the basis for our design we created a countertop with radial symmetry (detail A). This upended the traditional "golden triangle" of kitchen appliances, creating a more user friendly model where everything is reachable from anywhere around the hub. This symmetry also created a poetic relationship between the elevated hearth in the middle and the circular sink surrounding it.

After presenting to representatives from Caesarstone, my team's original design concept was chosen as the final design direction. In the next iteration we incorporated aspects from the other teams research into our design as well as our own new ideas: a biogas compressor for composting, an aquaponic tank on the dining table, automatic dispensors for ingredients, a slot based dish cleaner, steam atomizer jets for washing your hands, and a new upper exhaust section that incorporates magnetic storage, fiber optics, and LEDs.

In our third iteration we moved the aquaponic tank away from the dining table as it was decided it would not be appealing to have fish next to you while eating. We also incorporated an inclined vertical wall for the hydroponics, this allows harvesting even the highest plants easily.

The fourth iteration was again presented to representatives from Caesarstone, as well as other designers and a professional chef. Here the countertop has been divided into sections rather than the previous 360° elements. This allows for the internal systems to be tied together easier. This design was then revised according to the critics responses and sent to the fabricators.
